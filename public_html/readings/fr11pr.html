PRONONCIATION

Other Adjectives that Precede the Noun (Review and Expansion)

You have seen that some adjectives that precede masculine nouns have irregular written forms that begin with a vowel or a mute h:

Écoutez.

C'est un bel immeuble.

It's a beautiful apartment building.

C'est un nouvel immeuble.

It's a new apartment building.

C'est un vieil immeuble.

It's an old apartment building.

These irregular forms are pronounced like the corresponding feminine form of the adjective:

Écoutez.

C'est une belle maison.

It's a beautiful house.

C'est une nouvelle maison.

It's a new house.

C'est une vieille maison.

It's an old house.

Now examine the following sentences with the adjective bon, good, which also precedes the noun. Listen and repeat:

Écoutez.

1.

C'est un bon quartier.

It's a good neighborhood.

2.

C'est un bon appartement.

It's a good apartment.

3.

C'est un bon exercice.

It's a good exercise.

4.

C'est une bonne chambre.

It's a good room.

5.

C'est une bonne entreprise.

It's a good firm.

In which of the sentences does the adjective bon end in a nasal vowel?

How is the adjective bon pronounced in sentences 2 and 3?

How does that pronunciation compare with the pronunciation of the feminine form bonne in sentences 4 and 5?

How does the distribution of spoken (not written) forms parallel that of beau, nouveau, and vieux?

ANSWERS

The adjectives grand and gros also precede the noun, but in liaison, the masculine form is different from the feminine form. Examine the following phrases. Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.

un grand boulevard

a big boulevard

un gros sandwich

a thick sandwich

un grand ami

a great friend

un gros autobus

a huge bus

un grand homme

a great/tall man

un gros agenda

a big appointment book

une grande chambre

a big room

une grande idée

a great idea

une grosse balle

a big ball

une grosse erreur

a serious error

How is the final d of grand pronounced in liaison?

How is the final s of gros pronounced in liaison?

How do grand and gros before a masculine noun beginning with a vowel differ from the corresponding feminine forms in speech?

ANSWERS

Note that in the plural, the final s of all these adjectives is linked as z to the following noun. In the masculine, the adjective is pronounced as a masculine form before a plural noun. Listen and repeat:

Écoutez.

un bon ami

de bons amis

une bonne amie

de bonnes amies

un grand homme

de grands hommes

une grande amie

de grandes amies
