La Côte d’Ivoire et l’année scolaire

La Côte d’Ivoire

La Côte d’Ivoire, the Ivory Coast, a West African republic of about 15 million people, is located on the Gulf of Guinea between Liberia and Ghana. Its major city is the port of Abidjan, the heart of an urban area of over 2.5 million people. The capital of the region is Yamoussoukro, a city famous for the huge Basilica of Notre-Dame-de-la-Paix, a larger version of St. Peter’s in Rome. French traders began traveling to the Ivory Coast in the 14th century and colonized the region in 1842. Over a century later, in 1960, the Ivory Coast gained independence from France. While French is the official language of the country, as many as 60 different ethnic groups live in the Ivory Coast, each with its own language. The economy of the Ivory Coast is based on agriculture and oil. The country is also the world’s largest exporter of cocoa beans and the tenth largest exporter of coffee. Tourism is also important — 300,000 people visit the Ivory Coast each year.

L’an and l’année

French has two words for year: l’an(masculine) and l’année(feminine). Although they are sometimes interchangeable, there is a basic difference between the two. L’an focuses on the year as a point in time while l’annéefocuses on the year as a period in time. Thus you say, “Baudoin a quatre ans.” (“Baudoin is four years old.”), but “l’année scolaire” (“the school year”).
