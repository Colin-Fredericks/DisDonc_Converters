ANSWERS

The pronouns y and en follow affirmative command forms, as do all object and reflexive pronouns. Note that they precede negative command forms (as the other object and reflexive pronouns do).

N’y va pas.

N’y reste pas.

N’en parle pas.

N’en mange pas.

Before the pronouns y and en, the final s of the present tense forms is restored. (Note that in the negative commands, when y and en precede the verb, the final s is not restored – see the examples in the answer to question #1.)

The final s of the informal command forms that is written before the pronouns y and en undergoes liaison as /z/. Note that the final s (or z) of all command forms is linked as /z/ when the pronouns y and en follow directly.

Vas-y.

Allons-y.

Allez-y.

Manges-en.

Mangeons-en.

Mangez-en.
