The irregular forms bel, nouvel, and vieil are used only before masculine nouns beginning with a vowel. The regular feminine forms belle, nouvelle, and vieille are used before all feminine nouns, whether they begin with a vowel or a consonant: une belle affiche, une belle cuisine.

The irregular forms bel, nouvel, and vieil sound like the feminine forms belle, nouvelle, and vieille, in spite of the difference in spelling.

The demonstrative adjective also has a special form (cet) that is used before masculine nouns beginning with a consonant and that sounds like the feminine form (cette): ce salon, cet immeuble, cette affiche, cette cuisine.
