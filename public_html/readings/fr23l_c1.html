Le TGV

The TGV, le train à grande vitesse, has revolutionized transportation in Europe. The SNCF began creating the TGV rail network in the late 1960s. By the early 1980s, the Paris-Lyon line was operational and became an instant success. It reduced rail time between France’s two largest cities from nearly four hours to two hours and five minutes. The TGV eliminated the need for extensive air service between Paris and Lyon, since business travelers could now commute by rail between the downtown areas of the two cities. Since the 1980s, the SNCF has expanded service to include all of France, as well as Belgium, the Netherlands, Germany, Spain, Switzerland, and Italy. The construction of the tunnel under the English Channel has allowed for a 3 hour TGV rail connection between London and Paris.
