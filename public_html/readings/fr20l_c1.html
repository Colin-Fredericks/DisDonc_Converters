LANGUE ET CULTURE 1

Depuis un mois

French uses the present tense to express an action that began in the past and is still going on in the present. English uses has been doing something for this type of action.

Elle travaille avec nous depuis un mois.

She has been working with us for a month.

Avoir son âge

Note the phrase à peu près, almost or about, and the use of avoir in avoir mon âge, to be my age. Remember that avoir, not être, is used to express age in French.

Tu as quel âge?

How old are you?

J’ai vingt-trois ans.

I’m twenty-three.

Quel âge avez-vous?

How old are you?

J’ai trente ans.

I’m thirty.
