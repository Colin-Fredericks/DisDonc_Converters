Le Hameau

Le Hameau is, as the hotel owner says, an attempt to recreate a Swiss village of the past century. Around the reconstruction of the village core are upscale houses and apartments, boutiques, and cultural centers.



Banal

In feudal times, most Swiss villages had a communal oven - individual ovens were unusual. The word banal is a feudal term of Germanic origin. After the fall of the Roman Empire, France was a Germanic kingdom under the supervision of the Franks—in fact the name of the country, France, means “kingdom of the Franks”.) Ban referred to the territory under the jurisdiction of a sovereign. Banal originally meant of the jurisdiction, held in common by the people of the jurisdiction. The modern meaning of banal — dull, ordinary — dates back to the 18th century.

Le paysage

Verbier itself, over a mile high, is surrounded by dramatic Alpine peaks, including glaciers. Hiking in the area is rugged but offers spectacular views of the mountains, the village of Verbier, and the valley where Le Châble is located.
