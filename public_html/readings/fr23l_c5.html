LANGUE ET CULTURE 5

Car postal

In Switzerland, transportation between rail centers and remote villages is provided by a system of buses run by the post office. The bus stations for these lines are located at the post office of the village and they take passengers to the railway station. The car postal Régine is discussing in the Conversation links the Verbier post office in the center of the village with the railway station at Le Châble. The TGV also offers rail connections to all parts of Europe. The car postal stops in tiny towns between Verbier and Le Châble, thus assuring that people even in remote areas have convenient, inexpensive access to the rest of the country.

SNCF

SNCF is the abbreviation for Société nationale des chemins de fer français, the name of French national railroads.

Le funiculaire

Technically le funiculaire is a "rope railway" – a train that runs on tracks and is pulled by a cable. The precise term for this type of transportation is le téléphérique. Both le funiculaire and le téléphérique are used to climb steep slopes.

Gare de Lyon

The Gare de Lyon is one the several railroad stations serving Paris. Trains for the city of Lyon and other points to the southwest of Paris (such as Switzerland) leave from this station.

L’abbréviation CFF signifieChemins de fer fédéraux, the name of Swiss national railroads.
