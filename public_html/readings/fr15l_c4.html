LANGUE ET CULTURE 4

Nord, sud, est, ouest

The Northmen, known also as Vikings, were hardy seamen who invaded France in the 9th century, causing havoc and devastation across northern France. (They gave their name, la Normandie, to a prime agricultural region northeast of Paris where many of them settled.) The Vikings contributed many words to the French language, especially in the area of seafaring, including nord, sud, est, ouest.

Although the language of the Vikings was Norse, they assimilated to French culture and learned the French language. When the Normans invaded and conquered England in 1066 C.E., the Vikings’ language, Norman French, influenced the English language. This influence explains why the words for directions in French resemble the words for direction in English.
