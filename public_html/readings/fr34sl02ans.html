ANSWERS

 

   1.          In English subordinate clauses, the verb is always third-person singular or third-person plural.

 

   2.          In French subordinate clauses, all forms occur.

 

   3.          The English relative pronoun who always governs the third-person. The verb will be singular or plural, depending on the antecedent. The French relative pronoun qui takes on the person and number of the antecedent. Thus, in c’est moi qui, the relative pronoun qui requires the first-person singular (like the pronoun je) and the verb that follows and of which qui is the subject is also in the first-person singular: C’est moi qui suis inquiet.
