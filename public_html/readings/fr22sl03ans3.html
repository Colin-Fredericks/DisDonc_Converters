Yes, the two stems appear in the present subjunctive. The nous and vous forms use the -pren stem, the other persons use the -prenn stem. 

The singular of the present subjunctive keeps the final -nn of the stem (which it drops in the indicative and has only a nasal vowel). In addition, the subjunctive endings -e, -es, -e are used instead of the indicative endings -s, -s, -t. 

In the present indicative the third-person singular and third-person plural of the verb prendre are distinct in pronunciation: il prend vs. ils prennent. The final consonant of the stem is heard in the plural, but not in the singular. In the present subjunctive, the final consonant of the stem is heard in both the third-person singular and third-person plural, so the forms are identical: qu’il prenne vs. qu’ils prennent.
