PRONONCIATION

Question Intonation: Questions beginning with an Interrogative

In English and French, questions that begin with an interrogative have falling, not rising intonation. However, the pattern is somewhat different in the two languages. In English, these questions begin at a middle-level pitch on the question word, rise to high pitch on the stressed syllable and then drop. Listen to the speaker read the following English questions.

Écoutez.

What are you doing?

Where are you going?

Where is he from?

Why is he crying?

When does the movie begin?

At what time did the plane arrive?

How do you pronounce this?

Who’s knocking at the door?

In French, however, these questions begin at a high pitch on the interrogative and then there is a syllable-by-syllable drop in the pitch to the end of the sentence. Repeat after the speaker.

Écoutez.

Qu’est-ce que vous faites?

Où est-ce que vous allez?

D’où est-il?

Pourquoi il pleure?

Quand commence le film?

À quelle heure est-ce que l’avion est arrivé?

Comment est-ce que tu prononces ça?

Qui frappe à la porte?
