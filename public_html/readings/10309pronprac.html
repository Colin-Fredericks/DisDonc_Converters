PRONONCIATION

You have seen that certain final consonants in French are written but not pronounced unless the next word begins with a vowel. This phenomenon is known as liaison. Compare the following two sentences:

Écoutez.

Il est professeur.

C'est un professeur.

In the first sentence, the final t of estis not pronounced because the following word begins with a consonant. In the second sentence, the final t of estis pronounced because the following word begins with vowel. Listen to the sentences again.

Écoutez.

Il est professeur.

C'est un professeur.

Note that the t of c'est is pronounced as if it were the first consonant of the following syllable: c'es - tun.

A final s is always linked in plural determiners such as les, des, mes, tes, ses, nos, vos, leurs, and ces. Compare the following two phrases:

Écoutez.

des professeurs

des avocats

Note that when an s is linked, it is pronounced as a z. This z is pronounced as if it were the first consonant of the following syllable. Repeat the following phrases after the speaker. Pay close attention to the transcription of spoken syllables.

Écoutez.
