ANSWERS

The main clauses all express certainty about the reality of the event of the subordinate clause.

After these expressions the verb of the subordinate clause is in the indicative. Note that expressions such as être sûr/certain que and être evident/vrai que are followed by the subjunctive when negative and the indicative when positive. Note that être peu probable que acts like a negative clause and is followed by the subjunctive while être probable que is followed by the indicative.
