We're going to the grocery store.

Vocabulary Note

The official English translation for la charcuterie is the pork butcher's. In the past, le charcutier specialized in sausages while le boucher sold fresh meat. Today's charcuterie specializes not only in sausages but in ready-made foods such as salads, quiches, and soufflés as well as luncheon meats. The charcuterie is sometimes combines with the traiteur, caterer, who specializes in elaborate prepared dishes for business lunches, receptions, and dinners at home.

Vocabulary Note

The feminine nouns can designate either a woman who is one of these merchants or (traditionally) the wife of the merchant. Note that à la + shop name and chez + shopkeeper's name mean the same thing.
