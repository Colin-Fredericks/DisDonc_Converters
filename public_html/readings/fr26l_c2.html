LANGUE ET CULTURE 2

Se casser

Note the difference between casser qqch, to break something, which usually appears with a direct object (transitive verb), and se casser, to break, which has no direct object. Se casser is the equivalent of the intransitive meaning of the English to break.

J’ai cassé la valise.

I broke the suitcase.

La valise s’est cassée.

The suitcase broke.

Se faire rembourser

Se faire + infinitive means to get oneself, to get + verb.

se faire rembourser

to get a refund

se faire opérer

to be operated on, have an operation

se faire embaucher

to get hired

se faire mettre à la porte

to get fired

Composter

Passengers on French trains insert their tickets in a machine called a composteur that punches a hole in the ticket. The composteur is located at the entrance to each platform. The conductor on the train checks tickets for the punch, which makes the ticket valid for that day only.
