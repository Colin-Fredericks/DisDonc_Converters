The antecedent chambre is an inanimate noun. It refers to a thing. 

The antecedent colocataire is an animate noun. It refers to a person.

No, the choice of relative pronoun in French does not depend on whether the antecedent is animate or inanimate.

The choice of relative pronoun in French depends on the grammatical function of the relative pronoun in its clause.

If the relative pronoun is the subject of the verb of its clause, then qui is used:

J’ai une petite chambre qui coûte les yeux de la tête.

J’ai un colocataire qui est français.

If the relative pronoun is the object of the verb of its clause, then que is used:

Voici la chambre que je vais louer.

J’ai un colocataire que j’aime bien.
