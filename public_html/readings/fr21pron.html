PRONONCIATION

Liaison with nasals

When a nasal vowel appears before another vowel, the original nasal consonant represented in writing may be pronounced in liaison with the following vowel. In some words, the nasal vowel is retained, such as in the case of the possessive adjectives mon, ton, and son. (Remember that mon, ton, and son are used not only before all masculine nouns but also before feminine nouns beginning with a vowel.) Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.

mon ami

ton avenir

son éducation

mon université

ton oncle

son intention

In adjectives ending in a nasal vowel that come before the noun, the vowel is denasalized in liaison, giving the masculine form the same pronunciation as the feminine form. Compare and contrast the adjectives in the following groups. Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.

un bon professeur

une bonne directrice

une bonne institutrice

un bon instituteur

Écoutez.

un certain problème

une certaine formation

une certaine idée

un certain ennui

Écoutez.

le prochain feu rouge

la prochaine station

la prochaine autoroute

le prochain arrêt

Before a verb form beginning with a vowel, the pronoun en keeps the nasal vowel before the liaison of n. Listen and repeat.
