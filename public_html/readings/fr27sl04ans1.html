ANSWERS

In the French sentences, the prepositions appear before the interrogative. In the examples given, they occur as the first element of the sentences. Notice that in informal questions where the question word may appear elsewhere in the sentence, the preposition moves with it:

Tu manges avec qui?

Vous êtes d’où?

C’est pour quand, ce travail?

On sort par quelle porte?

In the colloquial English sentences, the prepositions are often separated from their objects and appear at the end of the sentence. Formal written English may move the prepositions to a position before the interrogative:

With whom are you eating?

By which door do we leave?
