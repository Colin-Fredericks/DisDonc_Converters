ANSWERS

English uses different pronouns. It uses him to refer to Danieland it to refer to dinner. English distinguishes between people and things.

English uses different pronouns. It uses her to refer to Éliane and it to refer to room. English distinguishes between people and things. 

French uses le to replace any masculine noun, whether that noun refers to people or things. Similarly, French uses la to replace any feminine noun, whether that noun refers to people or things. Thus, le corresponds to both him and it in English and la corresponds to both her and it.

In English, direct object pronouns follow the verb (She knowshim.) In French they precede the verb (Elle le connaît.)
