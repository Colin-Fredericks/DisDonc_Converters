LANGUE ET CULTURE 2

“Geler” et “crever”

The verbs geler, to freeze, be freezing, and crever, to burst, are conjugated like acheter. They have a variable stem, with an open è in the singular and the third person plural. This is shown in French spelling by changing the e of the infinitive to è.

je gèle

nous gelons

tu gèles

vous gelez

il/elle/on gèle

ils/elles gèlent

je crève

nous crevons

tu crèves

vous crevez

il/elle/on crève

ils/elles crèvent

Note that when used in slang or very informally, crever can be used instead of mourir, to die. This is a common student usage in France.

Jouer aux boules et faire du bowling

French distinguishes between jouer aux boules and faire du bowling, although both are usually translated into English as bowling. Les boules is a French game, similar to Italian bocci, that is played in parks and also professionally. Le bowling is the American game that is also played in France.
