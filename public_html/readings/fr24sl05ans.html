French sentences use the nouns retour and arrivée.

English uses the expressions when he got back and you to get here.

The possessive adjective son is the equivalent of the subject he in the first English sentence. The possessive adjective votre is the equivalent of the subject you in the second English sentence. 

The equivalent of the English conjunction "when" is the preposition à. This is logical because a conjunction is followed by the clause when he got back, but a noun is introduced by the preposition: à son retour.
