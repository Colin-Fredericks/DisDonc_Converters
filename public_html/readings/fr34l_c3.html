Sacré Cœur

 

The Sacré Cœur is one of the most famous Parisian landmarks. It overlooks the city of Paris from the Butte Montmartre, (Montmartre Hill), in the northern part of the city. Montmartre, once a village in the suburbs of Paris, became part of the city in the mid-19th century. Montmartre has long been known as the neighborhood of artists and bohemians. The Place du Tertre is the center of the varied nightlife of the area, due perhaps more to the influx of nightclubs and tourists than to actual artistic production. The suburban origins of the area can be seen in the many private houses with gardens, a rarity within the city limits of Paris.
