ANSWERS

The verbs in the main clause express doubt about the reality of the action in the subordinate clause.

The verbs croire and penser appear in the main clauses in the negative and interrogative forms.

After the negative and interrogative forms of croire and penser, the verb of the subordinate clause is in the subjunctive. Note that if the speaker wishes to express certainty about the state or action of the subordinate clause, he/she may use the indicative.

Je ne crois pas qu’ilvienne. /I don’t think he’s coming (but am not sure).

Je ne crois pas qu’ilvient. / I (really) don’t think he’s coming.
