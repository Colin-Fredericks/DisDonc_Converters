The Sound Represented by "u"

The sound represented by the letter u in French is produced by placing the tongue high and forward in the mouth as in English feed. However, for the French u, the lips are rounded and protruding. This combination of articulatory positions is not used in English.

Pronounce the following words after the speaker. As you say the vowel place your tongue in position for ee and round your lips, trying not to move your tongue back. The lips protrude more in French than they do for the sound represented by oo in English food.

The Sound Represented by "ou"

The French sound represented by ou is produced with the tongue arched high in the mouth, with the top of the arch at the back of the mouth. The lips are rounded and protruding. The closest sound in English is the oo of food, but there is a tendency in current American English to move the tongue forward when making that sound, so that it sometimes sounds like u to speakers of French.
