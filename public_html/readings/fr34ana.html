After an argument during which you called me a traitor? Oh, Paul, when I hear your voice ... If you want, you can come and we’ll talk about it. I’m at the office. Good-bye, Paul.

(34.19)

inventer

to invent; to make up (a story)

 

inventer une histoire de toutes pièces

to make up a story from start to finish, to tell a completely fabricated story

 

Le Louvre

The Louvre, originally built as the Palace of the Kings of France, is today a museum housing one of the greatest art collections in the world.

 

Comment? Au Louvre? Tu nous fais marcher, Baudoin. Tu inventes de toutes pièces.

What? To the Louvre? You’re putting us on, Baudoin. You’re making this up from start to finish.

 

 

(34.20)

I’m not making anything up. Solange said it over the phone. She said, “O.K., we’ll see each other at the Louvre then, at ten thirty."

 

 

(34.21)

(34.22)

C’est moi qui

I’m the one who

 

sortir

to take out

 

le tiroir

drawer

 

Elle a sorti quelque chose

Note the transitive use of sortir in this sentence. When sortir means to take out it has a direct object and is conjugated with avoir in the passé composé, not with être.

 

Non. Il est arrivé après. Il savait pas qu’elle y était allée. C’est moi qui le lui ai dit. Elle a sorti quelque chose de son tiroir et elle est partie.

No. He got here afterwards. He didn’t know she had gone there. I’m the one who told him. She took something out of her desk drawer and she left.
