LANGUE ET CULTURE 2

In the 6th and 7th centuries B.C., the Celts conquered parts of Asia and most of Europe, including what is now France. France was then known as “Gallia,” and although the region was eventually latinized, Celtic words remained part of the French language and are still used today. For example, le berceau, cradle, and brasser, to brew beer, are Celtic words, and several names of French cities, such as Paris, Lyon, Poitiers, and Amiens, are derived from Celtic tribal names or the names of fortified Celtic towns.

Before the Germanic invasion, Britain was also a Celtic-speaking country and, as a result, there are remnants of the Celtic language in English. The English word score reflects the Celtic system of counting by twenties as in four score and seven, which is equivalent to the French quatre-vingt-sept.
