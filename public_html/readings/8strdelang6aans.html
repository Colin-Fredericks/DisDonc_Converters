ANSWERS

In the French questions, the prepositions come at the beginning of the sentence, and before their objects (in these cases, the question words).

In the English sentences the preposition is separated from its object (the question word) and is placed at the end of the sentence.

In French, prepositions always come before their objects, whether those objects are nouns, pronouns, or question words. In English prepositions are often separated from their object when that object is a question word and is placed at the end of the question.
