ANSWERS

1.    The direct object nouns are bolded in the sentences below:

a.    Does Éliane know Daniel? Yes, she knows him

b.    Who’s preparing dinner? Éliane is preparing it.

c.    Does Madame Jolivet know Éliane? Yes, she knows her.

d.    Régine likes Éliane’s room, and she rents it.

e.    Someone puts the files on Éliane’s computer, and Mr. Jolivet sees them.

In sentence a, “Daniel” is the direct object of the verb know.

In sentence b, “dinner” is the direct object of the verb preparing.

In sentence c, “Éliane” is the direct object of the verb know.

In sentence d, “Éliane’s” room is the direct object of the verb likes.

In sentence e, “the files” is the direct object of the verb puts.

2.     The direct object pronouns are bolded:

a.     Does Éliane know Daniel? Yes, she knows him.

b.     Who’s preparing dinner? Éliane is preparing it.

c.     Does Madame Jolivet know Éliane? Yes, she knows her.

d.     Régine likes Éliane’s room, and she rents it.

e.       Someone puts the files on Éliane’s computer, and Mr. Jolivet sees them.

In sentence a, “him” is the direct object of the verb knows and refers to Daniel.

In sentence b, “it” is the direct object of the verb preparing.

In sentence c, “her” is the direct object of the verb knows and refers to Éliane.

In sentence d, “it” is the direct object of the verb rents and refers to the room.

In sentence e, “them” is the direct object of the verb sees and refers to the files.
