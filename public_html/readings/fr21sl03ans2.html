ANSWERS

The following quantity words beaucoup, trop, encore, assez, and plus appear in the above examples.

The following phrases that consist of de + noun are replaced by the reference pronoun en.

J’ai beaucoup de livres.

J’en ai beaucoup.

Tu as peu d’amis.

Tu en as peu.

Il a assez de problèmes.

Il en a assez.

The reference pronoun en is usually not translated in this context, although English sentences may use of them as an equivalent. However, the reference pronoun en is mandatory in French to refer back to a phrase consisting of de + noun. Note that the reference pronoun en can refer to both people and things with quantity words.

Le chef a renvoyé des informaticiens?

Did the boss fire any computer specialists?

Oui, il en a renvoyé beaucoup.

Yes, he fired a lot (of them).
