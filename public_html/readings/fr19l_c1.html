LANGUE ET CULTURE 1

Appeler

Appeler, like acheter, is a variable stem verb. The mute e of the infinitive changes to the è sound in the singular and the third-person plural of the present tense. In contrast to acheter, however, appeler reflects the sound by doubling the letter l, the final consonant of the stem, rather than by changing e to è.

J’appelle le médecin.

Nous appelons le médecin.

Tu appelles le médecin.

Vous appelez le médecin.

Il/Elle/On appelle le médecin.

Ils/Elles appellent le médecin.

Tomber

Note that the verb tomber forms the passé composé with être. In this Préparation, you learn two ways to say to break one’s arm. For example, to break one's arm translates as both se fracturer le bras and se casser le bras.

As in English, the term se fracturer, to fracture is generally a medical term, while se casser is typical of everyday speech.

Couvrir

Couvrir, like ouvrir, is a verb with an -ir infinitive that is conjugated like an -er verb in the present tense. The past participle of couvrir is couvert. Note that couvrir qqch de qqch means to cover something with something. (Couvrir la table d’une nappe, to cover the table with a tablecloth).

Je couvre la table d’une nappe.

Nous couvrons la table d’une nappe.

Tu couvres la table d’une nappe.

Vous couvrez la table d’une nappe.

Ils/Elles/On couvre la table d’une nappe.

Ils/Elles couvrent la table d’une nappe.

In the passé composé, the verb couvrir is conjugated as follows:

J’ai couvert la table d’une nappe.

Nous avons couvert la table d’une nappe.

Tu as couvert la table d’une nappe.

Vous avez couvert la table d’une nappe.

Ils/Elles/On a couvert la table d’une nappe.

Ils/Elles ont couvert la table d’une nappe.

Défendre

Note that the verb défendre has two meanings: to defend and to forbid. Similarly, the noun la défense means either defense or prohibition. Signs often reflect the latter usage. For example:

Défense de fumer, No smoking

Défense d’afficher, Post no bills
