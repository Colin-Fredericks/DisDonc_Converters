PRONONCIATION

Let's study the pronunciation of bœuf and œuf.

The final -f of these words is pronounced in the singular, but not in the plural. Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.

J'achète du bœuf.

I'm buying beef.

Voilà un bœuf.

There's an ox.

Voilà des bœufs.

There are oxen.

Les bœufs sont grands.

Oxen are big.

Écoutez.

Tu veux un œuf?

Do you want an egg?

Cet œuf est trop petit.

This egg is too small.

Tu veux des œufs?

Do you want eggs?

Une douzaine d'œufs, s'il vous plaît.

A dozen eggs, please.

Exercices de prononciation

Review the pronunciation of adjectives that come before nouns in the Prononciation section of the previous Préparation before doing the following practice activities.

Exercice 1: C'est bon.

Answer the speaker, saying that the item or person asked about is good. Use the adjective bon (good).

Modèle

Tu as des carottes?

Oui, j'ai de bonnes carottes.

Commençons.

Exercice 2: Le prochain

Answer the speaker's questions by saying that the item asked about is the next one. Use the adjective prochain (next).

Modèle

C'est quelle rue?

C'est la prochaine rue.

Commençons.

Exercice 3: Seulement des grands

The speaker asks you what these things and people are like. Say that each person or thing is big, using the adjective grand (big, great). Note that when preceding a noun that refers to people, grand usually means great rather than big.

Modèle

Elles sont comment, ces villes?

Ce sont de grandes villes.

Commençons.
