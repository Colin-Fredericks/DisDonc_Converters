PRONONCIATION

Sound and Spelling: é, er, ez

This section will help you learn to write the forms of the verbs when you hear them. Written French makes many more distinctions between verb forms than spoken French does, so it is important to concentrate on the various ways a single sound may be spelled, especially when those spelling differences signal grammatical functions.

Exercice 2

Listen to the following words. Which ones in each group contain a closed é?

Écoutez.

1.

cinéma __complète __cassette __étagère __géographie __bête

 

2.

chanter __habiter __monsieur __cahier __bonjour __fenêtre

 

3.

cherche __mangez __aiment __assez __invitez __entre

 

ANSWERS

Based on these exercises, you can see that the sound é in spoken French is used to signal three important verb forms:

the infinitive of -er verbs: habiter, inviter, regarder, arriver, retourner

the vous form of the present tense: mangez, vendez, partez, pensez

the past participle of -er verbs: trouvé, parlé, sonné, soupçonné, joué

The past participle will be discussed below.
