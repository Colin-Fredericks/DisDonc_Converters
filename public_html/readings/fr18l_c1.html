LANGUE ET CULTURE 1

Mener

The verb mener, to take someone away/off, is conjugated like acheter, to buy, and emmener, to bring. It has a variable stem, with an open è in the singular and the third person plural. This is shown in French spelling by changing the e of the infinitive to è. Listen to the conjugation of mener in the present and the passé composé.

je mène

nous menons

tu mènes

vous menez

il/elle/on mène

ils/elles mènent

je n’ai pas menti

nous n’avons pas menti

tu n’as pas menti

vous n’avez pas menti

il/elle/on n’a pas menti

ils/elles n’ont pas menti

Reconnaître

The verb reconnaître, to recognize, is conjugated like connaître, to know. It means both to admit and to recognize. Study the conjugation of reconnaître in the present and the passé composé.

je reconnais

nous reconnaissons

tu reconnais

vous reconnaissez

il/elle/on reconnaît

ils/elles reconnaissent

j’ai reconnu

nous avons reconnu

tu as reconnu

vous avez reconnu

il/elle/on a reconnu

ils/elles ont reconnu

Pouvoir

Note that the conditional form of pouvoir, to be able, is used in the Conversation. The conditional tense is used to indicate politeness as is seen in the request made of the taxi driver: Pourriez-vous. . . ., would you be able. . . .
