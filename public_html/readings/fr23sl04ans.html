No, the twenty-four hour clock counts the minutes past the hour from 1 to 59, much like a digital clock. When using the 24-hour clock, you cannot say quinze heures moins vingt. You say quatorze heures quarante. 

No, et quart, et demie, and moins le quart are not used in the 24-hour clock. The minutes are counted from 1 to 59. 

The sentence "Le vol 476 arrive à 9h38," can only refer to the morning. If the flight were arriving at 9:38 PM, the sentence would be, "Le vol 476 arrive à 21h38."
