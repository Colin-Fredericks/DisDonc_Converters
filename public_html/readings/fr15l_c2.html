LANGUE ET CULTURE 2

Cinq cents mètres

When directions are given in a U.S. city, distance is usually indicated by the number of blocks; the French usually express distance using the approximate number of meters. Phrases such as à cinquante mètres, à cent mètres, à trois cents mètres, and à cinq cents mètres are frequently heard in response to questions about the location of places. Keep in mind that a meter is just over three feet, so cent mètres is the approximate equivalent of one city block in the United States.

French does not have a word equivalent to the English block, because French cities are not laid out in a grid pattern as most American cities are. They are configured in a star-like pattern with large avenues radiating from large squares.
