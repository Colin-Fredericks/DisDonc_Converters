LANGUE ET CULTURE 2

La victime

The word la victime, like la personne, is always grammatically feminine, even when it refers to a male:

Une des victimes s’appelle Jean-Claude.

The name of one of the victims is Jean-Claude.

Les affaires

Note that the plural of affaire, les affaires, can mean one’s belongings, one’s things.

Où est-ce que je peux mettre mes affaires?

Where can I put my things.

Le bout

Note that French distinguishes between la fin, an abstract end (of a book, movie, trip), and le bout, which refers to a physical extremity. Thus, M. Jolivet met fin au stage d’Éliane, but Baudoin finds des bouts de papier. Notice the striking contrast between fin and bout.

la fin du monde

the end of the world (its destruction)

un voyage au bout du monde

a trip to the end of the world (very far away)

Au courant

In this lesson you will learn the use of the pronoun en which replaces phrases consisting of de + noun. Remember that this pronoun is usually not used with être au courant de quelque chose.

Vous êtes au courant de la situation?

Are you up to date about the situation?

Non, je ne suis pas au courant.

No, I’m not informed about it.
