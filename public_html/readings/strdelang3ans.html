ANSWERS

  1. The preposition de connects the item owned with the name of the owner.
  1. In English, the possessor precedes the thing possessed. In French, the thing possessed comes first and is connected to the possessor by the preposition de.
  1. The preposition de becomes d’ before a vowel. This is another example of élision. Compare je + ai = j’ai; le + ordinateur = l’ordinateur; and la + imprimante = l’imprimante.
