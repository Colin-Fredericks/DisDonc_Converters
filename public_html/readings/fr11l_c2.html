LANGUE ET CULTURE 2: MAUVAIS/BON, AVOIR TORT / AVOIR RAISON

Le mauvais sac

Since the words right and wrong have so many meanings in English, you have to be careful when expressing them in French. Two simple rules will help:

Before a noun, bon and mauvais are used:

C’est le bon café?

Is this the right café?

Non, c’est le mauvais.

No, it’s the wrong one.

Le bon café est en face.

The right café is across the street.

When referring to people, use avoir raison, to be right, and avoir tort, to be wrong.

J’ai toujours raison, moi.

I’m always right.

Mais maintenant tu as tort.

But now you are wrong.
