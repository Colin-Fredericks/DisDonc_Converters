LANGUE ET CULTURE 1

Le/la colocataire

The term camarade de chambre is really a translation of the English word roommate and can be used to refer to dorm-mates in the U.S. The most current French term for someone you share an apartment with is le/la colocataire, a term that literally means co-tenant.

La salle de bains

The toilet and the bathroom are typically separated in France. The term W.C. is the abbreviation of the English water closet. It is usually pronounced “les vé-cé” and sometimes called “les water,” pronounced with a French accent as if it were a French word: “ouatère.” To ask for the location of the toilet in public places you say, “Où sont les W.C., s’il vous plaît?” or “Où sont les toilettes, s’il vous plaît?”

Armoire

The word armoire in French usually refers to a free-standing closet or piece of furniture designed to hold clothing. The term for a walk-in closet is la penderie.
