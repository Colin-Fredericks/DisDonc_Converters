PRONUNCIATION PRACTICE 1

Basic Intonation Patterns (1)

Intonation is the rise and fall of the voice in speaking. The intonation pattern of a sentence helps determine its meaning. For instance, say these sentences aloud:

Is that a cassette recorder?That's a cassette recorder.

Did your voice rise or fall at the end of the question: "Is that a cassette recorder?"

Did your voice rise or fall at the end of the statement: "That's a cassette recorder."?

Now listen to the speaker pronounce these French statements.

Écoutez.

C'est un magnétophone.

J'ai un billet de dix.

Le garçon apporte le déjeuner.

Did the speaker's voice rise or fall at the end of the statements?

Now listen to the speaker pronounce these French questions.

Écoutez.

C'est ton voisin?

Vous avez des sandwichs?

Nous sommes en retard?

Did the speaker's voice rise or fall at the end of the questions?

French and English have parallel intonation systems - the voice falls in statements, but rises in questions, such as C'est ton voisin?, that can be answered with either oui or non. As you practice new material, you should try to imitate the speaker's intonation as well as his or her pronunciation.
