LA STRUCTURE DE LA LANGUE 1: LES VERBES PRONOMINAUX EXPRIMANT L’IDÉE DE RÉCIPROCITÉ

Study the following sentences from the conversation and un peu plus. Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.

On s’est connu l’autre jour.

We met the other day.

On peut s’écrire par courrier électronique.

We can write to each other by e-mail.

On peut se passer un coup de fil.

We can call each other.

On se retrouve à sept heures.

We’ll meet at seven o’clock.

Vous vous êtes donné rendez-vous?

Did you make an appointment (with each other)?

Ils s’aiment beaucoup.

They like each other a lot.

Are the subjects of the above sentences singular or plural? What kind of verbs are they?

What meaning do all the above verbs share?

Note that agreements in the passé composé follow the same rule as with all reflexive verbs. The past participle agrees with the reflexive pronoun if the reflexive pronoun is a direct object, but not if it is an indirect object. Verbs in which the reflexive pronoun is an indirect object are marked with an asterisk in the above list.

Compare:

Ils se sont parlé.

They spoke to each other.

Ils se sont vus.

The saw each other.

LA STRUCTURE DE LA LANGUE 3: L’EMPLOI DU FUTUR APRÈS LA CONJONCTION "QUAND"

Compare the following sentences in French and English. Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.

On mangera quand on aura faim.

We’ll eat when we are hungry.

The detectives will continue the investigation until the day when they find the stolen files.

Passe-moi un coup de fil dès que tu seras prête.

Give me a call as soon as you are ready.

How does the use of tenses in the subordinate clauses of the above sentences differ in French and English?

Can you formulate a rule for the use of the tenses in French in sentences like these?

LA STRUCTURE DE LA LANGUE 4: LES VERBES COMME "MANQUER," ET "IL RESTE"

Sometimes verbs in French and English have very different structures. Compare the following sentences in French and English.

Écoutez.

Tu me manques terriblement.

I miss you terribly.

Les enfants veulent que je rentre. Je leur manque.

The children want me to return home. They miss me.

What are the subjects of the French sentences with the verb manquer?

What are the subjects of the English sentences?

What role do the words corresponding to the English subjects play in the French sentences?

ANSWERS

In the French verb manquer,to miss someone, the subject of the English sentence becomes the indirect object, and the object of the English sentence appears as the subject. Study the following examples. Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.

Tu me manques.

I miss you.

Il me manque.

I miss him.

Elle me manque.

I miss her.

Vous me manquez.

I miss you.

Ils/Elles me manquent.

I miss them.

Écoutez.

Je te manque.

You miss me.

Il te manque.

You miss him.

Elle te manque.

You miss her.

Nous te manquons.

You miss us.

Ils/Elles te manquent.

You miss them.

Écoutez.

Je lui manque.

He misses me.

Tu lui manques.

He misses you.

Il lui manque.

He misses him.

Elle lui manque.

He misses her.

Nous lui manquons.

He misses us.

Vous lui manquez.

He misses you.

Ils/Elles lui manquent.

He misses them.

Écoutez.

Je leur manque.

They miss me.

Tu leur manques.

They miss you.

Il leur manque.

They miss him.

Elle leur manque.

They miss her.

Nous leur manquons.

They miss us.

Vous leur manquez.

They miss you.

Ils/Elles leur manquent.

They miss them.

The verb rester, to be left or to have something left, always appears in the third-person singular in the impersonal expression il reste. An indirect object is used to indicate the person that would be the subject of the English sentence.

Study the following sentences. Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.

Il me reste 300 francs.

I have three hundred francs left.

Il te reste une heure.

You have an hour left.

Qu’est-ce qui lui reste?

What does he have left?

Il lui reste sa maison.

She is left with her house.

Il nous reste trois cours.

We have three courses left.

Il ne vous reste pas de temps.

You have no time left.

Il leur reste un ami à Paris.

They have a friend left in Paris.

LA STRUCTURE DE LA LANGUE 5: LES SUBSTANTIFS QUI REMPLACENT UNE PROPOSITION

French often uses a noun where English uses a subordinate clause. When a subordinate clause in English expresses motion, French stylistic usage often prefers a noun phrase.

Écoutez.

Je l’ai vu à son retour.

I saw him when he got back.

Écoutez.

Nous attendons votre arrivée.

We’re waiting for you to get here.

In the above sentences, which nouns express motion?

How do the English sentences express the same ideas?

What is the French equivalent of the subjects of the verbs of motion found in the English sentences?

What takes the place of the conjunction "when" in the French sentence?

ANSWERS
