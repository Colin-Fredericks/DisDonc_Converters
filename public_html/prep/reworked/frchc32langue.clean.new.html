La Structure De La Langue 1: Le Verbe "S’En Aller"

The verb s’en aller means to go away, leave. It is conjugated like aller, but is always pronominal (reflexive) and always contains the pronoun en.

Study the present tense of s’en aller.

Je m’en vais au marché.
Nous nous en allons au marché.
I’m leaving to go to the market.
We’re leaving to go to the market.
Tu t’en vas au marché.
Vous vous en allez au marché.
You’re leaving to go to the market.
You’re leaving to go to the market.
Il/Elle/On s’en va au marché.
Ils/Elles s’en vont au marché.
He/She/One is leaving to go to the market. / We are leaving to go to the market.
They’re leaving to go to the market.

The command forms include the reflexive pronoun and the pronoun en.

Va-t’en.
Go away.
Allons-nous-en.
Let’s go away. / Let’s leave.
Allez-vous-en.
Go away.

In the compound tenses, s’en aller is conjugated with être. The reflexive pronoun is considered a direct object, and the past participle agrees with it.

Je m’en suis allé(e) sans faire de bruit.
Nous nous en sommes allé(e)s sans faire de bruit.
I left without making any noise.
We left without making any noise.
Tu t’en es allé(e) sans faire de bruit.
Vous vous en êtes allé(e)(s) sans faire de bruit.
You left without making any noise.
You left without making any noise.
Il s’en est allé sans faire de bruit.
Ils s’en sont allés sans faire de bruit.
He left without making any noise.
They left without making any noise.
Elle s’en est allée sans faire de bruit.
Elles s’en sont allées sans faire de bruit.
She left without making any noise.
They left without making any noise.
On s’en est allé(e)(s) sans faire de bruit.
One/We left without making any noise.

Study the pluperfect of s’en aller.

Je m’en étais allé(e) tôt.
Nous nous en étions allé(e)s tôt.
I had left early.
We had left early.
Tu t’en étais allé(e) tôt.
Vous vous en étiez allé(e)(s) tôt.
You had left early.
You had left early.
Il s’en était allé tôt.
Ils s’en étaient allés tôt.
He had left early.
They had left early.
Elle s’en était allée tôt.
Elles s’en étaient allées tôt.
She had left early.
They had left early.
On s’en était allé(e)(s) tôt.
One/We had left early.

The present subjunctive of s’en aller is based on the subjunctive of aller.

Il faut que je m’en aille .
Il faut que nous nous en allions .
I have to leave.
We have to leave.
Il faut que tu t’en ailles .
Il faut que vous vous en alliez .
You have to leave.
You have to leave.
Il faut qu’il/elle/on s’en aille .
Il faut qu’ils/elles s’en aillent .
He/She/One has to leave. / We have to leave.
They have to leave.
Tout le monde doute que je m’en sois allé(e) .
Tout le monde doute que nous nous en soyons allé(e)s .
Everyone doubts that I left.
Everyone doubts that we left.
Tout le monde doute que tu t’en sois allé(e) .
Tout le monde doute que vous vous en soyez allé(e)(s) .
Everyone doubts that you left.
Everyone doubts that you left.
Tout le monde doute qu’il s’en soit allé .
Tout le monde doute qu’ils s’en soient allés .
Everyone doubts that he left.
Everyone doubts that they left.
Tout le monde doute qu’elle s’en soit allée .
Tout le monde doute qu’elles s’en soient allées .
Everyone doubts that she left.
Everyone doubts that they left.
Tout le monde doute qu’on s’en soit allé(e)(s) .
Everyone doubts that we left.

La Structure De La Langue 2: Le Subjonctif Après Les Conjonctions Adverbiales

Certain conjunctions in French are followed by the subjunctive:

à moins que
unless
avant que
before
bien que
although
de peur que
for fear that
jusqu’à ce que
until
pour que
in order that, so that
pourvu que
provided that
sans que
without

Study the following examples.

Les copains récupéreront les disquettes à moins que M. Toësca les ait vendues.
The friends will get back the diskettes unless M. Toësca has sold them.
Il faut lui parler avant qu’il s’en aille.
We have to speak to him before he leaves.
Ils savent qu’il faut agir bien qu’ils aient peur de M. Toësca.
They know that they have to act although they are afraid of Mr. Toësca.
Ils hésitent à lui parler de peur qu’il soit armé.
They are hesitant to speak to him for fear that he may be armed.
On ne bougera pas d’ici jusqu’à ce qu’il dise oui.
We won’t move from here until he says, “yes.”
Explique-le-moi pour que je le comprenne.
Explain it to me so that I’ll understand.
Nous partirons demain pourvu que nous puissions récupérer les disquettes.
We’ll leave tomorrow provided that we can get the diskettes back.
Approchons-nous de lui sans qu’il s’en rende compte.
Let’s get closer to him without his realizing.

Note that in formal literary style, the conjunctions à moins que, avant que, and de peur que often have a ne before the verb. We have seen this in subordinate clauses following verbs expressing fear, such as avoir peur que and craindre que.

Il partira à moins que son frère ne revienne.
He’ll leave unless his brother returns.
Avertissez-nous avant qu’ils ne s’en aillent.
Notify us before they leave.
Nous ne disons rien de peur que le patron ne se mette en colère.
We’re not saying anything for fear that the boss might get angry.

Note that a pleonastic ne does not make the clause negative.

In everyday French après que, after, is also followed by the subjunctive, although in formal language the indicative is required.

La Structure De La Langue 4: Les Mots Facilement Confondus

Douter and se douter

Douter means to doubt. It is followed by the subjunctive when the statement is affirmative and by the indicative when the statement is negative.

Je doute qu’il revienne.
I doubt that he’s coming back.
Je ne doute pas qu’il reviendra.
I don’t doubt that he’s coming back.

 

Se douter de qqch means to suspect something. When se douter is in the positive, or negative interrogative, and is followed by a clause, the clause is usually in the indicative.When se douter is in the negative or interrogative, the clause that follows is usually in the subjunctive.

Je me doutais que tu t’en rendais compte.
I suspected that you realized it.
Je ne me doutais pas que tu t’en sois rendu(e) compte.
I didn’t suspect that you realized it.
Te doutais-tu qu’il s’en soit rendu compte?
Did you suspect that he realized it?
Ne te doutais-tu pas qu’il s’en rendait compte?
Didn’t you suspect that he realized it?

Penser de and penser à

The phrase penser de is used to express an opinion.

Que pensez-vous de la situation actuelle?
What do you think of the current situation?

The phrase penser à qqn / penser à qqch means to have someone or something in mind.

Je pense souvent à mon lycée.
I often think of my high school.
Je pense aux amis que j’y avais.
I think of the friends I had there.

Animate nouns that follow penser de and penser à are replaced by the preposition + stressed pronoun.

Tu penses à tes parents?
Do you think of your parents?
Oui, je pense tout le temps à eux.
Yes, I think of them all the time.

Partir and s’en aller

Partir and s’en aller both mean to leave. Partir is more commonly used to refer to the duration of trips and vacations, and implies that the person leaving will return.

Il est parti en voyage d’affaires. Il reviendra dans dix jours.
He left on a business trip. He’ll return in ten days.

The verb s’en aller focuses on the act of leaving and does not necessarily imply a return.

Je sais qu’il n’est pas là. Il s’en est allé.
I know he’s not here. He went away.

S’en aller (but not partir) can therefore be used as a euphemism for to die, equivalent in tone to the English to pass away.

Le malade s’en est allé doucement.
The patient passed away peacefully.
