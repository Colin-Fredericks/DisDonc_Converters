La Structure De La Langue 1: L’Emploi Des Subjonctif Après L’Expression Des Sentiments

Use of the subjunctive is required in subordinate clauses following expressions of emotion. In these cases the subjunctive indicates the speaker’s subjective reaction rather than the objective fact of the occurrence of the action.

Je suis content que tu puisses venir.
I’m glad that you can come.
Il est furieux que vous ne soyez pas prêts.
He’s furious that you’re not ready.

The expression of emotion can be a verb:

Je crains que
I’m afraid that
J’aime que
I like the fact that

or an adjective:

Je suis content que
I’m happy that

or an impersonal expression beginning with il est que or c’est que:

Il est bon que
It’s good that
Il est mauvais que
It’s bad that
Il est triste que
It’s sad that
Il est merveilleux que
It’s wonderful that
Il vaut mieux que
It’s better that

Expressions of emotion are closely related to main clause phrases that express a subjective judgment in that they both employ the subjunctive in the subordinate clause.

Il est important que
It’s important that
Il est essentiel que
It’s essential that
Il est nécessaire que
It’s necessary that
Il est indispensable que
It’s indispensable that
Il est préférable que
It’s preferable that
Il est utile que
It’s useful that

The opposites of these expressions are also followed by the subjunctive:

Il n’est pas nécessaire que
It’s not necessary that
Il est inutile que
It’s useless that

La Structure De La Langue 2: Le Futur

The future tense is formed in French by adding a special set of endings to the infinitive of the verb. The r sound appears in all forms of the future tense. Study the future tense of arriver. Listen and repeat the following sentences. Pay special attention to the r sound.

J’ arriverai le mois prochain.

Nous arriverons le mois prochain
We’ll arrive next month.
Tu arriveras le mois prochain.
You’ll arrive next month.
Vous arriverez le mois prochain.
You’ll arrive next month.
Il/Elle/On arrivera le mois prochain.

Ils/Elles arriveront le mois prochain.
They’ll arrive next month.

Several verbs have irregular stems in the future. In these verbs, the future stem is not derived directly from the infinitive. However, even these irregular stems end in r. The endings are the same for all verbs in the future.

Avoir and Savoir have future stems in aur-:

Avoir
j’aurai, tu auras, etc.
Savoir
je saurai, tu sauras, etc.

Être and Faire have future stems in -er:

Être
je serai, tu seras, etc.
Faire
je ferai, tu feras, etc.

Voir and envoyer have future stems in -err -:

Voir
je verrai, tu verras, etc.
Envoyer
j’enverrai, tu enverras, etc.

Verbs with infinitives in -evoir drop the oi in the future:

Devoir
je devrai, tu devras, etc.
Recevoir
je recevrai, tu recevras, etc.

Courir and mourir drop the i of the infinitive, leaving a double r, which is pronounced in speech.

Courir
je courrai, tu courras, etc.

Questions:

  1. Which of the two stems is used in the future tense? The stem of the infinitive with mute e or the stem of the infinitive with open e?
  2. What determines the choice of the stem in the future tense?
  3. Which subject forms have different stems in the present and the future?
  4. Which spelling would you expect to find in the future tense for verbs ending in -yer? The letter y or i?
Show Answers
  1. The stem with open e is used.
  2. The stem with open e is used before a syllable containing a mute e. In all the forms of the future, the e of the infinitive of -er verbs is pronounced as a mute e.
  3. The nous and vous forms use the stem with mute e in the present, but the stem with open e in the future. Thus, the variable stem verbs, like all other verbs, have the same stem in all forms of the future.
  4. All the future forms of verbs ending in -yer end in i, not y, because these verbs change y to i before a mute e, and in all the forms of the future, the e of the infinitive of -er verbs is pronounced as a mute e: je balaierai, nous nettoierons, vous essuierez.

Now look at the forms of préférer in the present and the future. Listen and repeat in pairs.

je préfère

Note that verbs like préférer use the infinitive stem in all forms of the future, in spite of the fact that future tense forms have a mute e in the following syllable.

The future tense is not the only tense that expresses future time in French. The present tense can express future time if there are other elements of the sentence that refer to the future.

Nous partons demain pour la Suisse.
We’re leaving for Switzerland tomorrow.

On descend à la prochaine station.
We’re getting off at the next station.

Note that English often uses the present progressive (are leaving, are getting off) in these cases.

Aller + Infinitive (le futur proche)

Je vais vous apporter un café.
I’ll bring you a cup of coffee.
Il va s’asseoir dans le salon.
He’s going to sit down in the living room.

Note that in French the aller + infinitive construction and the future tense are not equivalents. The aller + infinitive construction stresses that the action mentioned will soon be completed.

The future tense is less specific in describing the completion of an action. Thus, if a friend at your house says, "J’ai faim," your response should be "Je vais te donner du pain et du fromage". Using the response, "Je te donnerai du pain et du fromage" might make the friend wonder how long he is going to have to wait for something to eat.

When adverbs like maintenant refer to the immediate future, the futur proche must be used.

Maintenant on va manger.
Now we will eat.

However, when an adverb of time referring to the future is used, the futur proche and the future tense are virtually indistinguishable.

On va partir demain
We’ll leave tomorrow.
On partira demain.
We’ll leave tomorrow.

La Structure De La Langue 3: Ne... Que

You have already learned the French word seulement, only. Another very common way to say only is to place ne before the verb and que before the word that only qualifies.

Après, il n’y a que le taxi, et c’est cher.
Afterwards, there’s only the taxi, and it’s expensive.
Baudoin n’a trouvé que deux petits bouts de papier.
Baudoin found only two little pieces of paper.
Je n’ai que ma carte de crédit.
All I have is my credit card.

The structure ne... que is not treated as a negative construction, so the partitive article is reduced to de following que.

Tu as des magazines?
Do you have any magazines?
Non, je n’ai que des journaux.
No, I have only newspapers.

In the absence of a verb, the word que before a noun often means only.

Il y a du fromage?
Is there any cheese?
Que du pain.
Just bread.

Tu as des francs?
Do you have any francs?
Que des euros.
Only euros.

Qu’est-ce que tu as vu dans sa chambre?
What did you see in his room?
Que des livres.
Just books.

Ils ont des preuves?
Do they have proof?
Que des soupçons.
Just suspicions.

The expression n’avoir qu’à faire qqch means all one has to do is.

Qu’est-ce qu’il faut faire pour connaître les horaires du TGV?
What do you have to do to find out the schedules of the TGV?
Tu n’as qu’à taper sncf.com.
All you have to do is type sncf.com.

Le chef n’est pas satisfait du travail de cet employé.
The boss is not satisfied with this employee’s work.
Il n’a qu’à le mettre à la porte.
He should just fire him.

La Structure De La Langue 4: L’Heure Officielle

In formal circumstances, (train, bus and air schedules, and event times) French uses a twenty-four hour clock. This system removes the need for using AM and PM. In the twenty-four hour clock, 12:01 o'clock noon is 12h and midnight is 24h. Here are some examples of the use of the 24-hour clock for transportation and movie schedules. Listen and repeat.

Est-ce qu’il y a un TGV pour Marseille à 14h15?
Is there a TGV to Marseilles at 2:15 PM?
Non, mais il y en a un à 15h30 et un autre à 16h56.
No, but there’s one at 3:30 PM and another at 4:56 PM.

Est-ce qu’il y a une séance du film à 17h, s'il vous plaît?
Excuse me. Is there a showing of the film at 5:00 PM.
Non, nos séances sont à 19h et à 21h20.
No, we’re showing the film at 7 PM and at 9:20 PM.
Est-ce que la séance de 21h20 est la dernière?
Is the 9:20 PM showing the last one?
Non, aujourd’hui il y a une séance à 23h45.
No, today there is a showing at 11:45 PM.

Questions:

  1. Does the twenty-four hour clock express minutes from thirty-one after the hour using moins?
  2. Does the twenty-four hour clock designate parts of the hour with et quart, et demie, moins le quart?
  3. If you call an airport in France and you are told, "Le vol (flight) 476 arrive à 9h38", do you ask if the plane is arriving in the morning or evening?
Show Answers
  1. No, the twenty-four hour clock counts the minutes past the hour from 1 to 59, much like a digital clock. When using the 24-hour clock, you cannot say quinze heures moins vingt. You say quatorze heures quarante.
  2. No, et quart, et demie, and moins le quart are not used in the 24-hour clock. The minutes are counted from 1 to 59.
  3. The sentence "Le vol 476 arrive à 9h38," can only refer to the morning. If the flight were arriving at 9:38 PM, the sentence would be, "Le vol 476 arrive à 21h38."

Note culturelle: The Misleading Face of the French Economy

The article below was written in 1999 as part of the original textbook. It has been left in its original form.

When conversing on the subject of their country today, the French are more likely to wax enthusiastic about their culture than they are to extol the strengths of its economy. And yet in 2023, France is the seventh mightiest economic power in the world today (behind the United States, China, Japan, Germany, India, and the UK). French business enterprises have played a major role in bringing about the phenomenon of global free trade; and, for the past ten or so years, thanks to a positive balance of trade, France has been able to amass enough working capital to finance a commercial expansion into foreign countries. Moreover, France is opening its doors to the numerous foreign investors who are eager to take advantage of its central position within the European Union.

Though to some extent marginalized, French agriculture continues to occupy a more than symbolic place within the nation's economic structure. Largely an exporting activity, it deals chiefly in upscale products, such as wines. Capitalizing on financial subsidies of European origin, it has achieved considerable diversity in terms of products, size, and types of business operation.

If French industry periodically experiences an erosion of prestige, it remains substantial, particularly in an era of mergers and acquisitions. Corporate enterprises employ approximately 35% of the nation's workers and include several world or European leaders. In 2023, the Franco-Belgian TotalEnergies is the third largest world oil concern in terms of production and reserves. The Renault-Nissan-Mitsubishi Alliance is the world's largest light vehicle manufacturer. Aventis, born from the merger of Rhône-Poulenc with the German Hoechst Corporation, is a world leader in various proprietary medical products. Aérospatiale, the designer of Airbus planes and the Ariane air shuttle, is headquartered in Toulouse. Lafarge is the leading maker of construction materials in the world; Saint-Gobain ranks among the top three in the sphere of specialty materials (notably, glass). Usinor is preeminent among the world's leading iron and steel groups, and is the foremost European one. France's banking sector, still fairly insular, is forging new alliances at the European level. LVMH is the primary group among the world's top companies specializing in luxury goods, while L'Oréal is number one in the cosmetics domain. In the realm of new technologies, France is admittedly dwarfed by American industries, but has some competitors of very high quality: the Franco-Italian ST Microelectronics is the largest European semiconductor manufacturer; Capgemini is fourth among computer data-processing companies; and Infogrames Entertainment ranks second in the design of video games. In addition, as France happens to be the most visited country in the world, its tourism industry has contributed to the resurgent dynamism of the economy at the end of the 1990s.

French employers tend generally to emerge from the ranks of the Grandes Écoles , but a few have come up the ranks as “self-made men” based on the American model. Among these, François Pinault is without a doubt the one with the most impressive success story. A true capitalist, he has switched businesses, partners, and entourages frequently and today, at age sixty-four, has amassed the country's second largest fortune (valued at approximately 5 billion euros in 1999).Self-taught, the son of a forestry operator, he started out in the wood business and is now the head of a veritable empire that he directs more and more towards new technologies.

In a paradox of the new economic structure, French business ventures find themselves weakened to some degree by their very success. With more and more foreigners as shareholders, they have inevitably become vulnerable to the impact of the sudden mood changes from Wall Street.

Note culturelle: Le visage méconnu de l’économie française

L'article ci-dessous a été écrit pour le manuel original en 1999. Il est présenté sous sa forme originale.

De la France, on parle aujourd’hui plus volontiers de sa culture que de son économie. Pourtant, elle est aujourd’hui la septième puissance économique mondiale ( derrière les États-Unis, le Chine, le Japon l’Allemagne, l'Inde, et l'UK) et ses entreprises prennent une large part au phénomène de mondialisation des échanges. Grâce à une balance commerciale excédentaire depuis une dizaine d’années, elle a pu accumuler des capitaux permettant à ses entreprises de financer leur expansion dans les pays étrangers. La France est aussi terre d’accueil de nombreux investissements étrangers, qui profitent de sa position centrale dans l’Union européenne.

Marginalisée, l’agriculture occupe pourtant encore une place plus que symbolique dans l’économie française. C’est une activité largement exportatrice, notamment dans les produits haut-de-gamme comme les vins. Bénéficiant de subventions d’origine européenne, elle est très diversifiée en terme de produits, de taille et de type d’exploitation.

L’industrie a vu son poids reculer régulièrement, mais reste encore très importante (environ 35% des emplois), et comporte quelques champions mondiaux ou européens, surtout après les vagues de fusions et d’acquisitions récentes. Ainsi, le groupe franco-belge TotalEnergies est le quatrième groupe pétrolier mondial en termes de production et de réserves. Le constructeur automobile Renault qui a racheté le japonais Nissan est le quatrième mondial, et prévoit de racheter le coréen Samsung pour se développer dans les marchés émergents d’Asie. Aventis, né de la fusion de Rhône-Poulenc avec l’allemand Höchst, est un leader mondial dans certaines spécialités pharmaceutiques. Aérospatiale, le concepteur des avions Airbus et des fusées Ariane, est basé à Toulouse. Lafarge est le premier groupe mondial de matériaux de construction, et Saint-Gobain est dans les trois premiers dans le domaine des matériaux spécialisés (verre notamment). Usinor est le deuxième groupe sidérurgique mondial et le premier européen. Le secteur bancaire est encore très fermé («franco-français»), mais des alliances au niveau européen devraient avoir lieu très bientôt. Le groupe LVMH est le premier groupe mondial dans le secteur du luxe, et L’Oréal est numéro 1 dans le domaine des cosmétiques. Dans le domaine des nouvelles technologies, la France est dominée par le poids des groupes américains, mais compte quelques concurrents de qualité: le franco-italien ST Microélectroniques est huitième mondial dans le domaine des microprocesseurs, Capgemini est le quatrième groupe de conseil en informatique, et Infogrames Entertainment est deuxième dans la conception de jeux vidéos. La fièvre de l’Internet gagne rapidement la France, qui rattrape son retard sur les États-Unis dans ce domaine. Par ailleurs, la France est le pays le plus visité au monde, et le secteur du tourisme contribue au dynamisme retrouvé de l’économie à la fin des années 1990.

Le patronnat français est pour l’essentiel composé d’anciens élèves des Grandes Écoles, mais comporte aussi quelques «self-made men» à l’américaine. Parmi ceux-ci, François Pinault est sans doute celui qui a mené sa barque le plus loin. En vrai capitaliste, il a changé plusieurs fois de métiers, d’entourage, de partenaires et aujourd’hui, à soixante-quatre ans, il a accumulé la deuxième fortune du pays, évaluée en 1999 à environ 5 milliards d’euros. Autodidacte, fils d’un exploitant forestier, il a commencé dans le commerce du bois pour aujourd’hui se retrouver à la tête d’un véritable empire qu’il oriente de plus en plus vers les nouvelles technologies.

Paradoxalement, les entreprises françaises sont aujourd’hui un peu fragilisées par leur succès puisque leurs actionnaires sont essentiellement étrangers, ce qui les laisse à la portée de la moindre saute d’humeur venue de Wall Street.
