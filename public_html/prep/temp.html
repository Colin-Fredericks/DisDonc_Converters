LA STRUCTURE DE LA LANGUE 1: PLUS-QUE-PARFAIT

The pluperfect tense indicates what happened before another past action took place. It consists of the imperfect of the auxiliary verbs avoir or être + the past participle. The English equivalent of the pluperfect is had done something.

Verbs conjugated with avoir:

Écoutez.

J’avais ouvert le bureau à 8h.

Nous avions ouvert le bureau à 8h.

I had opened the office at 8:00.

We had opened the office at 8:00.

Tu avais ouvert le bureau à 8h.

Vous aviez ouvert le bureau à 8h.

You had opened the office at 8:00.

You had opened the office at 8:00.

Il/Elle/On avait ouvert le bureau à 8h.

Ils/Elles avaient ouvert le bureau à 8h.

He/She/One had opened the office at 8:00.

They had opened the office at 8:00.

Verbs conjugated with être:

Écoutez.

J’étais arrivé(e) avant le chef.

Nous étions arrivé(e)s avant le chef.

I’d arrived before the boss.

We’d arrived before the boss.

Tu étais arrivé(e) avant le chef.

Vous étiez arrivé(e)s avant le chef.

You’d arrived before the boss.

You’d arrived before the boss.

Il/Elle/On était arrivé(e)(s) avant le chef.

Ils/Elles étaient arrivé(e)s avant le chef.

He’d/She’d/One had arrived before the boss.

They’d arrived before the boss.

Pronominal verbs:

Écoutez.

Je m’étais déjà réveillé(e).

Nous nous étions déjà réveillé(e)s.

I had already woken up.

We had already woken up.

Tu t’étais déjà réveillé(e).

Vous vous étiez déjà réveillé(e)(s).

You had already woken up.

You had already woken up.

Il/Elle/On s’était déjà réveillé(e)(s).

Ils/Elles s’étaient déjà réveillé(e)s.

He/She/One had already woken up.

They had already woken up.

Pronominal verbs where the reflexive pronoun is an indirect object:

Écoutez.

Je m’en étais déjà rendu compte.

Nous nous étions déjà rendu compte.

I had already realized.

We had already realized it.

Tu t’en étais déjà rendu compte.

Vous vous en étiez déjà rendu compte.

You had already realized it.

You had already realized it.

Il/Elle/On s’en était déjà rendu compte.

Ils/Elles s’ en étaient déjà rendu compte.

He/She/One had already realized it.

They had already realized it.

Rules of agreement for the past participle are the same as in the passé composé.

LA STRUCTURE DE LA LANGUE 2: PRONOM DE RAPPEL "Y"

You have seen that the choice of object pronouns in French depends on the underlying structure of the verb. If the object noun is connected to the verb directly, without a preposition, it is replaced by a direct object pronoun (le, la,les).

Écoutez.

Tu as accompagné Éliane?

Did you go with Éliane?

Oui, je l’ai accompagnée.

Yes, I accompanied her.

Écoutez.

Vous avez écrit ces articles?

Did you write these articles?

Oui, je les ai écrits.

Yes, I wrote them.

Animate nouns (nouns referring to living beings) connected to nouns by the preposition à are replaced by lui and leur.

Écoutez.

Vous avez parlé au directeur?

Did you speak to the director?

Non, je ne lui ai pas parlé.

No, I didn’t speak to him.

Écoutez.

Qu’est-ce qu’il a donné aux enfants?

What did he give to the children?

Il leur a donné des bonbons.

He gave them candy.

French has other object pronouns that are best designated by the French term pronom de rappel,reference or recall pronouns. These pronouns replace sequences of prepositions + noun that are not replaced by pronouns in English. Examine the following sentences. Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.

Éliane répond aux questions?

Does Éliane answer the questions?

Baudoin et Théophile jouent au foot?

Are Baudoin and Théophile playing soccer?

Tu penses à ce message?

Are you thinking about that message?

What construction do the boldfaced phrases consist of?

Are the nouns animate or inanimate?

ANSWERS

Now look at these questions and possible answers. Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.

Éliane répond aux questions?

Does Éliane answer the questions?

Oui, elle y répond.

Yes, she does. (She answers them.)

Baudoin et Théophile jouent au foot?

Are Baudoin and Théophile playing soccer?

Oui, ils y jouent.

Yes, they are. (They’re playing it.)

Tu penses à ce message?

Are you thinking about that message?

Non, je n’y pense plus.

No, I’m not thinking about it any more.

Are the boldfaced phrases replaced by the indirect object pronouns lui and leur?

What pronoun replaces the boldfaced phrases?

ANSWERS

Now look at the following pair of questions and answers. Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.

Tu comptes répondre à sa lettre?

Do you intend to answer his letter?

Oui, je compte y répondre.

Yes, I’m going to answer it.

Écoutez.

Tu vas répondre au professeur?

Are you going to answer the teacher?

Oui, je vais lui répondre.

Yes, I’m going to answer him.

What rule can you formulate for the use of y as opposed to lui and leur?

ANSWER

Note that in the expression penser à qqn, the phrase à + animate noun is not replaced by an indirect object pronoun but by a stressed pronoun.

Tu penses souvent à ta fiancée?

Do you often think of your fiancée?

Oui, je pense toujours à elle.

Yes, I’m always thinking of her.

Note that the sentence "Je lui pense," is grammatically incorrect in French.

Now look at the following sentences. Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.

Is Grandmother in the garden?

(there)

Yes, she’s watering the flowers.

Écoutez.

Il n’y a rien sur le sofa.

There’s nothing on the sofa.

Bon. Tu peux y mettre les paquets.

Good. You can put the packages (on it).

Écoutez.

Qu’est-ce qu’elle fait à l’aéroport?

What is she doing at the airport?

Elle y travaille.

She works there.

Écoutez.

Où est le chien? Sous la table?

Where’s the dog? Under the table?

Oui, il s’y est endormi.

Yes, he fell asleep there.

What do the phrases dans le jardin, sur le sofa, à l’aéroport, and sous la table express?

What pronoun replaces them in the answer to each question?

ANSWERS

Écoutez.

Tu retournes au bureau?

Are you going back to the office?

Oui, j’y vais maintenant.

Yes, I’m going there now.

Écoutez.

Tu vas en ville?

Are you going into town?

Pas aujourd’hui. J’y vais demain.

Not today. I’m going (there) tomorrow.

Note that the rules for position of the reference pronoun y are the same as those for object pronouns. In the following sentence, the reference pronoun y precedes the conjugated verb.

Nous y prenons un thé.

We’re having tea there.

But in the following sentence, y comes between the verb and an infinitive.

Nous pouvons y prendre un thé.

We can have tea there.

LA STRUCTURE DE LA LANGUE 3: PRONOM DE RAPPEL "EN"

You have seen how the reference pronoun y replaces prepositional phrases beginning with à or a preposition of location such as sur,sous, dans, en, and chez. Examine the following sentences. Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.

Vous voulez du café?

Do you want any coffee?

Merci, je n’en bois jamais.

No thank you, I never drink any.

Écoutez.

L’entreprise cherche des programmeurs?

Is the firm looking for programmers?

Oui, elle en cherche.

Yes, they are looking (for some).

Écoutez.

Baudoin a des jouets?

Does Baudoin have toys?

Oui, il en a.

Yes, he has (some).

What does the reference pronoun en replace?

Does the reference pronoun en replace both animate and inanimate nouns?

Does the reference pronoun en always have an English equivalent?

ANSWERS

Now look at the following exchanges. Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.

Baudoin a beaucoup de jouets?

Does Baudoin have a lot of toys?

Son père dit qu’il en a trop.

His father says he has too many (of them).

Écoutez.

Tu vas acheter encore des disquettes?

Are you going to buy more diskettes?

Non, j’en ai assez.

No, I have enough.

Écoutez.

Tu prends encore des frites?

Are you having any more French fries.

Merci, je n’en veux plus.

No thanks. I don’t want any more.

After what kind of words do the de phrases appear in the above examples?

How are these phrases replaced?

Does the reference pronoun en have an English equivalent in these contexts?

ANSWERS

Now look at the following exchanges. Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.

Baudoin a trouvé trois bouts de papier?

Did Baudoin find three bits of paper?

Non, il en a trouvé deux.

No, he found two.

Écoutez.

M. Jolivet a laissé deux messages?

Did Mr. Jolivet leave two messages?

Non, il en a laissé un.

No he left one.

Écoutez.

Est-ce que tu as ouvert quinze fichiers?

Did you open fifteen files?

Non, j’en ai ouvert dix.

No, I opened ten.

Écoutez.

Le chef a embauché un ingénieur?

Did the boss hire an engineer?

Il en a embauché trois.

He hired three.

What does the reference pronoun en replace in the above examples?

Does the reference pronoun en have an English equivalent in these contexts?

Does the past participle agree with the reference pronoun en when it comes before the verb?

ANSWERS

Note that the reference pronoun en is used to replace nouns following de in fixed verbal expressions such as avoir peur de or se souvenir de. Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.

Tu as peur des hôpitaux?

Are you afraid of hospitals?

Non, je n’en ai pas peur.

No, I’m not afraid of them.

Écoutez.

Tu te souviens de leur adresse?

Do you remember their address?

Non, je ne m’en souviens pas.

No, I don’t remember it.

However, animate nouns are not replaced by en with these verbs. The preposition de + the stressed pronoun is used instead (de lui,d’elle, d’eux, d’elles).

Écoutez.

Tu te souviens de ma copine ivoirienne?

Do you remember my friend from the Ivory coast?

Non, je ne me souviens pas d’elle.

No, I don’t remember her.

Note also that the encan be used to replace phrases referring to the starting point that begin with de,from, with verbs such as venir, revenir,and sortir.

Écoutez.

Le chef est revenu de New York?

Has the boss come back from New York?

Oui, il en est revenu hier.

Yes, he came back (from there) yesterday.

Écoutez.

Vous avez été au bureau?

Were you at the office?

J’en sors.

I’m just coming from there. (I was just there.)

LA STRUCTURE DE LA LANGUE 4: LES PRONOMS "Y" ET "EN" AVEC LES VERBES PRONOMINAUX

Since many pronominal verbs are connected to an object noun by means of the prepositions à and de, the pronouns y and en often appear with them. When the pronouns y and en are used with pronominal verbs, they follow the reflexive pronoun. The reflexive pronouns me, te, and se undergo élision and become m’y/m’en, t’y/t’en, and s’y/s’en in combination with the pronouns y and en.

Some examples:

Je me trouve dans le jardin.

Je m’y trouve.

Je me rends compte du problème.

Je m’en rends compte.

Tu t’intéresse à ces idées.

Tu t’y intéresses.

On se doute de sa malhonnêteté.

On s’en doute.

Ils se sont fait mal au bras.

Ils s’y sont fait mal.

Vous vous méfiez de ses messages.

Vous vous en méfiez.
