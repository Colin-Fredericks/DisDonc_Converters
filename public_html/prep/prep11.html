Prononciation

Other Adjectives that Precede the Noun (Review and Expansion)

You have seen that some adjectives that precede masculine nouns have irregular written forms that begin with a vowel or a mute h:

C'est un bel immeuble.
It's a beautiful apartment building.
C'est un nouvel immeuble.
It's a new apartment building.
C'est un vieil immeuble.
It's an old apartment building.

These irregular forms are pronounced like the corresponding feminine form of the adjective:

C'est une belle maison.
It's a beautiful house.
C'est une nouvelle maison.
It's a new house.
C'est une vieille maison.
It's an old house.

Now examine the following sentences with the adjective bon, good, which also precedes the noun. Listen and repeat:

1.

Answers

The adjective bon in sentences 2 and 3 is pronounced like the feminine form bonne in sentences 4 and 5.

La Structure De La Langue 1: Les Verbes

Acheter et espérer

Many verbs in French do not use the same vowel in the stem all the way through the conjugation of the present tense. In these verbs, the three singular forms and the third-person plural have a different vowel from that of the nous and vous forms. In this section you will learn some -er verbs with variable stems.

In the verb acheter,to buy, the mute e changes to an open è in all the forms of the singular and also in the third-person plural. Listen and repeat.

j’ach è te
nous ach e tons
tu ach è tes
vous ach e tez
il/elle/on ach è te
ils/elles ach è tent

Now study the conjugation of the verb espérer, to hope. Listen and repeat.

j’esp è re
nous esp é rons
tu esp è res
vous esp é rez
il/elle/on esp è re
ils/elles esp è rent

What is the vowel of the stem in the infinitive?

In what forms of the conjugation is this vowel maintained?

In what forms of the conjugation does this vowel change? What does it change to?

Show Answers

Answers

The vowel of the stem in the infinitive is é.

The vowel é appears in the nous and vous forms.

In the three forms of the singular and in the third-person plural, the stem vowel é changes to è. Note that this is not just a spelling change but also a change in pronunciation. Pay attention to the pronunciation changes in espérer and acheter as you repeat the forms after the speaker.

Note that in the verb préférer, to prefer, the first é is part of the prefix pré and does not change because it is not the stem vowel. The é of the stem fér changes to è in the singular forms and also in the third-person plural. Listen and repeat.

La Structure De La Langue 2: Les Verbes

Prendre, apprendre, comprendre

Study the conjugation of the verb prendre, to take. Listen and repeat.

je prends
nous prenons
tu prends
vous prenez
il/elle/on prend
ils/elles prennent

Note that the stem of the verb prendre ends in n. This n drops in the singular, leaving a nasal vowel sound. Remember that the loss of the final consonant of the stem in the pronunciation of the singular is characteristic of the formation of the present tense for all French verbs whose infinitives do not end in er.

Compare the following example and listen to the way the d sound in the plural form drops in the singular to form a nasal vowel sound.

ils descendent
il descend

Notice how the pronunciation of the vowel changes in the nous and the vous forms. Listen and repeat.

nous prenons
vous prenez

The third-person plural form ends in the consonant n, which is preceded by the oral vowel è. The third-person singular form ends in a nasal vowel. Listen and repeat.

ils prennent
il prend

Thus, prendre is both a variable stem verb, like acheter, and a non -er verb in which the final consonant of the stem is dropped in the pronunciation of the singular form.

The endings of prendre in the singular resemble those of -re verbs. They follow the pattern of verbs that do not end in er: the ending s is used for je and tu; the ending t is used for il, elle, and on unless the stem ends in d or t, in which case the ending t is not added. This explains the conjugation of the singular:

je prend s
tu prend s
il /elle/on prend

L'emploi de “prendre”

Prendre is the equivalent of the English to take:

Elle prend son argent et descend faire les courses.
She takes her money and goes down to do the shopping.

Prendre is the equivalent of the English to have when used with items of food and drink.

Qu’est-ce que tu prends le matin? Du café ou du thé?
What do you have in the morning? Coffee or tea?
Je prends un chocolat chaud.
I have hot chocolate.

For example, to describe meals, you say the following:

prendre le petit déjeuner
to have breakfast
prendre le déjeuner
to have lunch
prendre le dîner
to have dinner

Prendre is used with means of transportation as follows:

Je prends le train pour Paris.
I’m taking/catching the train to Paris.
Pour voir Paris, tu peux prendre le bus.
To see Paris, you can take the bus.
Il prend le Métro pour aller à la fac.
He takes the subway to go to school.
Ils prennent l’avion pour New York.
They take the plane for New York.

Prendre is not used to express to take a course or to study a subject. Use faire instead.

Elle fait de la biologie.
She’s taking biology.

La Structure De La Langue 3: Le Présent De “Pouvoir”

La conjugaison

The verb pouvoir, can or to be able to, is irregular. Listen and repeat.

Je peux descendre maintenant.
Nous pouvons descendre maintenant.
Tu peux descendre maintenant.
Vous pouvez descendre maintenant.
Il/Elle/On peut descendre maintenant.
Ils/Elles peuvent descendre maintenant.

Pouvoir is a variable stem verb. What is the variation in the stem?

How is pouvoir like other verbs that do not have an infinitive ending in er?

The endings of pouvoir in the singular resemble those of verbs that do not end in er. Can you explain how?

Show Answers

Answers

The nous and vous forms have the same vowel as the infinitive:

pouvoir

nous pouvons

vous pouvez

The singular and the third-person plural have the vowel eu:

je peux

tu peux

il/elle/on peut

ils/elles peuvent

Pouvoir loses the final consonant of its stem in the singular. Compare the forms ils peuvent and il peut.

The ending s in French is often written x after the combinations of letters au, eu,and ou. For example, the plural of nouveau is nouveaux. Thus, the final x of the forms je peux and tu peux is a spelling variation for the ending s.

L'emploi de “pouvoir”

The verb pouvoir is usually followed by an infinitive.

Est-ce que tu peux déménager samedi?
Can you move on Saturday?
Non, samedi j’ai cours. Je peux déménager dimanche.
No, Saturday I have classes. I can move on Sunday.

In the negative, ne precedes the form of pouvoir, and pas follows it directly, coming between pouvoir and the infinitive:

Nicole ne peut pas dîner chez nous?
Can’t Nicole have dinner at our house?
Non, elle ne peut pas venir. Elle n’a pas de voiture.
No, she can’t come. She doesn’t have a car.

The negative of pouvoir is not usually used in French before verbs such as trouver, to find, or voir,to see.

Je ne trouve pas mes lunettes.
I can’t find my glasses.
Je ne vois pas les petits mots sans mes lunettes.
I can’t see the little words without my glasses.

Be sure not to pronounce the e of ne in the negative of pouvoir after je, tu, nous, and vous. Listen and repeat, paying close attention to how the speaker pronounces ne.

Je ne peux pas descendre avec Éliane.
Tu ne peux pas descendre avec Éliane.
Nous ne pouvons pas descendre avec Éliane.
Vous ne pouvez pas descendre avec Éliane.

Answers

The final consonant sound of the stem of venir is n.

This final n drops in the singular, leaving a nasal vowel. Listen again to the pronunciation of the three singular forms.

je viens

L'emploi de “venir”

The verb venir means to come.

Tu viens manger chez nous?

They come to see Éliane.

La Structure De La Langue 8: Le Verbe + L’Infinitif

In French, sometimes two verbs are used together in a sentence. The first verb often expresses an opinion regarding the second verb, which is in the infinitive form. Some verbs are connected directly to the following infinitive, without any connecting word. Study the sentences below.

Tu peux venir demain?
Can you come tomorrow?
Nous voulons parler avec toi.
We want to talk with you.
On va prendre le goûter ensemble.
We’ll have afternoon snack together.
J’espère trouver mon sac.
I hope to find my backpack.
Mes copains préfèrent dîner avec nous.
My friends would rather have dinner with us.
Je n’ose pas écrire davantage.
I don’t dare write more.
Vous comptez louer une chambre?
Do you intend to rent a room?
Tu aimes voyager?
Do you like to travel?
Je déteste manger à la cantine.
I hate eating in the cafeteria.
Baudoin adore jouer au football.
Baudoin loves to play soccer.

Thus, the verbs pouvoir, vouloir, aller, espérer, préférer, oser, compter, aimer, détester, and adorer are followed directly by the infinitive.

Now look at the following verb + infinitive constructions:

Je vous invite à dîner.
I invite you to have dinner.
Il commence à comprendre.
He’s beginning to understand.
Tu viens de dîner?
Have you just had dinner?
Tu as peur de voyager seul?
Are you afraid to travel alone?

What word connects inviter and commencer to the infinitive?

What word connects venir and avoir peur to the infinitive when it has the meaning to have just done something?

Can you express the difference in meaning between Tu viens dîner? and Tu viens de dîner?

The word à connects inviter and commencer to the infinitive.

The word de connects venir and avoir peur to the infinitive when it has the meaning to have just done something.

Tu viens dîner? means Are you coming to have dinner? and Tu viens de dîner? means Have you just had dinner?

20. Read the Note culturelle on Vichy France

Note Culturelle

Vichy France

In 1940, France, having lost monumental struggles against Nazi Germany, decided to sign an armistice with Hitler. The armistice allowed for the division of France into two zones: the North Zone, occupied by the Germans, and the South Zone, which was theoretically free and under French jurisdiction. It was decided that the capital of the South Zone would be Vichy, a temperate, trendy city in the center of France. The expression "Vichy" or "Vichy France" is used to describe the government in France during World War II.

Delegates met at Vichy and voted to give full constitutional power in France to Marshal Pétain, a French military leader and World War I hero, immediately putting an end to the Republic and creating l'État français, the French State. Pétain became the head of the French State and with the help of his advisor, Pierre Laval, implemented his politics of révolution nationale, which were founded on the very conservative platform of "work, family, country." Syndicates were banned, all opposition or democratic activity was prohibited, and the government deliberately collaborated with Nazi Germany. Vichy government organized roundups and assisted the Germans in deporting French Jews. The Vichy regime hunted and condemned to death both Nazi Resistants and Communists.

In 1944 France was liberated and the Vichy government collapsed. Pétain had to flee to Germany while Charles de Gaulle (leader of the French Free Forces during World War II) and the Resistants took power in Paris. After the defeat of Germany, Pétain and Laval were brought back to France to be tried for high treason. During the trial, Pétain defended himself by arguing that his goal was not to collaborate with Germany, but to minimize the damage of the war for France. He was found guilty and condemned to death. De Gaulle decided to pardon Pétain, however, because of his age and his heroic participation in World War I. Pétain died in 1951.

More than an episode of history in the course of a tragic period, the Vichy regime still remains a subject of great debate and controversy in France. For a long time, no one wanted to remember the crimes of Vichy, which were committed in tacit agreement by a majority in France. Vichy's participation in the "Final Solution" (the plan of the German Third Reich to exterminate all Jews) was hidden or downplayed for a long time because the French people were ashamed. Recently, trials against former civil servants of Vichy have taken place, and many of the civil servants have been condemned for crimes against humanity.
