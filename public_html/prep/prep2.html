Langue et Culture 1

French has two forms for you: tu and vous. Tu is a familiar, or informal, form. Daniel uses tu to address Régine because he guesses she is a student like him and because she is his age. Students and other young people can use tu to address each other even if they haven’t met before.

French is the official language not only of France, but also of three other European countries: Belgium, where it is co-official with Dutch; Switzerland, where it is co-official with German, Italian, and Romanch; and Luxembourg, where it is taught in the primary grades along with Letzeburgish (the national language) and German. French is also the official language of multiple countries in Africa, including the D.R. Congo, Monaco, Niger, and Senegal. It is co-official with English in Canada, and with Haitian Creole in Haiti.

Here are the approximate populations of French speakers in a few selected countries as of 2020 [1]:

Français Écrit / Français Parlé

Here are some clues to understanding the French writing system:

As you have seen, most consonants that appear at the end of a word in writing are not pronounced: étudiant. In speech, it ends in a nasal vowel, even though in writing it ends in a t. Other examples: chocolat, vous

You will also have noticed that the final s that is added in writing to form the plural is not pronounced: allemands, italiennes, chinoises

A final e after the last consonant of a French word usually indicates that the preceding consonant is pronounced: chinoise.

In which of the following words is the last consonant pronounced?

copine, pardon, française, vous, Lambert, allemande

The syllable that sounds like the first syllable of English water is written oi in French spelling: moi. Can you think of at least two other French words that have the letters oi representing this sound?

The letters ou in French represent a sound similar to the oo in English food, but pronounced with the lips protruding and the tongue arched far back in the mouth: vous. The letter u represents a very different sound, a vowel formed by saying the ee of keen, while rounding your lips without moving your tongue: une.

Structure de la Langue 1

Subject Pronouns: “tu” vs. “vous”

Remember that French has two words for you: tu and vous. The choice between them depends on factors such as the relationship between the speakers and their ages.

Tu, a singular form, is used when you are speaking to a person with whom you have an informal relationship: a friend, a fellow student, a parent, or a child. It is also used to address an animal.

Vous is used to address a person you don’t know well, an older person, or a stranger. Vous is also used when addressing more than one person, no matter what the relationship is. Vous is thus both singular and plural.

French culture tends to be more formal than U.S. culture, and French-speaking people use tu much less than Americans use first names. When in doubt, use vous. An inappropriate tu can be rude and even offensive.

Structure de la Langue 2

The Verb “être,” to be

Learn the forms of être with the subject pronouns that accompany them. The forms of the verb change for each subject pronoun. This is called agreement because the verb agrees with its subject.

French English
je suis I am
tu es you are
il est he is
elle est she is
nous sommes we are
vous êtes you are
ils sont they are
elles sont they are

Note that in both French and English, each form consists of a subject pronoun and a verb form.
