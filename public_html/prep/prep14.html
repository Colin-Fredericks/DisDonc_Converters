La Structure De La Langue 1: Heure, Temps, Fois

Compare these English and French sentences.

Écoutez.
Time is money. Le temps, c’est de l’argent.
What time is it? Quelle heure est-il?
We changed trains three times. Nous avons changé trois fois de train.

Which sentence refers to clock time?

Which sentence refers to a specific number of occurrences?

Which sentence refers to an abstract idea rather than a specific time?

Show Answers

Answers

Quelle heure est-il?The word heure means time as in clock time, and also means hour: Nous attendons depuis deux heures. (We have been waiting for two hours.)

Nous avons changé trois fois de train.The word fois means time in the sense of number of occurrences: une fois means once; deux fois means twice; trois fois means three times.

Le temps, c’est de l’argent.Recall from the discussion on On a le temps (We have time.), ways to express time in the abstract. Note that combien de fois means how many times while combien de temps means how much time or how long.

La Structure De La Langue 3: Le Présent Des Verbes En “Yer”

Verbs whose stems end in yer are regular except for a spelling change. Examine the present tense forms of essayer, to try. Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.
J’essaie de comprendre. Nous essayons de comprendre.
Tu essaies de comprendre. Vous essayez de comprendre.
Il/Elle/On essaie de comprendre. Ils/Elles essaient de comprendre.

Which forms have a spelling change?

What does the spelling change consist of?

Can you formulate a rule for the spelling change?

Show Answers

Answers

The spelling change takes place in the singular and in the third-person plural.

The spelling change consists of y changing to i.

In verbs ending in yer, y changes to i before a mute e. The endings of the singular and the third-person plural all have a mute e.

Here are some common verbs ending in yer:

balayer to sweep
essayer to try
essayer de faire qqch to try to do something
payer to pay
employer to use
ennuyer to bore
essuyer to wipe
nettoyer to clean
tutoyer to address someone in the tu form
vouvoyer to address someone in the vous form

Note that verbs ending in ayer, such as essayer and payer, are sometimes written with the letter y in all forms: je paye, tu payes, etc. However, verbs ending in oyer and uyer always make the change of y to i before a mute e.

La Structure De La Langue 5: Le Présent Des Verbes Comme “Sortir”

The verb sortir means to go out. It is the antonym of entrer, to enter. Study its conjugation. Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.
Je sors à trois heures. Nous sortons à trois heures.
Tu sors à trois heures. Vous sortez à trois heures.
Il/Elle/On sort à trois heures. Ils/Elles sortent à trois heures.

How do the plural forms of sortir differ from those of -ir verbs such as finir?

What is the stem of the verb sortir?

What happens to the final consonant of the stem in the singular forms of sortir?

What are the endings of the singular form?

Show Answers

Answers

The plural forms of sortir (nous sortons, vous sortez, ils/elles sortent) do not have the iss of verbs like finir: nous finissons, vous finissez, ils/elles finissent.

The stem of the verb sortir is sort. The stem does not end in s as it does for verbs like finir whose stem is finiss.

The final consonant of the stem (t) drops in thesingular forms: je sors, tu sors, il sort.

The endings of the singular are s, s,and t, as in most verbs whose infinitive does not end in er.

Other verbs like sortir:

dormir to sleep
partir to leave (on a trip)
servir to serve

Study the conjugations of these verbs. Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.
Je dorsbien. Nous dormons bien.
Tu dors bien. Vous dormez bien.
Il/Elle/On dort bien. Ils/Elles dorment bien.
Écoutez.
Je pars en voyage. Nous partons en voyage.
Tu pars en voyage. Vous partez en voyage.
Il/Elle/On part en voyage. Ils/Elles partent en voyage.
Écoutez.
Je sers le café. Nous servons le café.
Tu sers le café. Vous servez le café.
Il/Elle/On sert le café. Ils/Elles servent le café.

Which of the three verbs is conjugated exactly like sortir?

What is the final consonant of the stem of dormir?

What is the final consonant of the stem of servir?

Show Answers

Answers

Partir is conjugated exactly like sortir because the final consonant of its stem is t.

The final consonant of the stem of dormir is m. This m drops in both speech and writing in the singular forms.

The final consonant of the stem of servir is v. This v drops in both speech and writing in the singular forms.

La Structure De La Langue 6: Le Passé Composé Des Verbes Réguliers

So far all the verbs you have used in Dis donc! have been in the present tense. Now you will learn how to express the past tense in French. Study the following pairs of sentences. Listen and repeat.

Écoutez.
Je travaille à la bibliothèque. I study in the library.
J’ai travaillé à la bibliothèque. I studied in the library.
Écoutez.
Vous remplissez les verres. You are filling the glasses.
Vous avez rempli les verres. You filled the glasses.
Écoutez.
Il perd ses clés. He loses his keys.
Il a perdu ses clés. He lost his keys.

How many elements are there in present-tense verb forms?

How many elements are there in past-tense verb forms?

Can you recognize what the first element of the past-tense forms is?

Show Answers

Answers

Present-tense verb forms have a single element: travaille, remplissez, perd.

Past-tense verb forms have two elements each: ai travaillé, avez rempli, a perdu.

The first element of the past-tense forms is the present tense of the verb avoir (to have).

The tense most often used to refer to events in the past in French is a compound tense consisting of an auxiliary verb (avoir) and a form of the verb called the “past participle,” or le passé composé. In the next Préparation you will see that some verbs use être rather than avoir to form the passé composé.

The past participle does not change to agree in gender and number with the subject of the verb when avoir is the auxiliary verb. The past participle is formed from the stem of regular verbs, as follows:

Note that verbs ending in yer have y and not i in the past participle, because the ending é is not a mute e: payé, nettoyé, essuyé.
