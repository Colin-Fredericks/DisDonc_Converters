Sound and Spelling: é, er, ez

This section will help you learn to write the forms of the verbs when you hear them. Written French makes many more distinctions between verb forms than spoken French does, so it is important to concentrate on the various ways a single sound may be spelled, especially when those spelling differences signal grammatical functions.

Based on these exercises, you can see that the sound é in spoken French is used to signal three important verb forms:

  1. the infinitive of -er verbs: habiter, inviter, regarder, arriver, retourner
  2. the vous form of the present tense: mangez, vendez, partez, pensez
  3. the past participle of -er verbs: trouvé, parlé, sonné, soupçonné, joué

The past participle will be discussed below.

La Structure De La Langue 1: Heure, Temps, Fois

Compare these English and French sentences.

Time is money.
Le temps, c’est de l’argent.
What time is it?
Quelle heure est-il?
We changed trains three times.
Nous avons changé trois fois de train.

Questions:

  1. Which sentence refers to clock time?
  2. Which sentence refers to a specific number of occurrences?
  3. Which sentence refers to an abstract idea rather than a specific time?
Show Answers
  1. Quelle heure est-il? The word heure means time as in clock time, and also means hour: Nous attendons depuis deux heures. (We have been waiting for two hours.)
  2. Nous avons changé trois fois de train.The word fois means time in the sense of number of occurrences: une fois means once; deux fois means twice; trois fois means three times.
  3. Le temps, c’est de l’argent.Recall from the discussion on On a le temps (We have time.), ways to express time in the abstract. Note that combien de fois means how many times while combien de temps means how much time or how long.

Questions:

  2. What happens to the final consonant of the stem in the singular forms?
  3. What are the endings of the singular forms of lire in writing?
  4. The present tense of lire is similar to the present tense of dire, except in one of the six forms. Which form is different?
Show Answers
  1. The final consonant is s, pronounced z.
  2. The final consonant drops in the singular. All singular forms are pronounced li.
  3. The endings are s, s, and t. These endings are not pronounced.
  4. The vous form of dire is irregular: vous dites.

La Structure De La Langue 3: Le Présent Des Verbes En “Yer”

Verbs whose stems end in yer are regular except for a spelling change. Examine the present tense forms of essayer, to try. Listen and repeat.

J’essaie de comprendre.
Nous essayons de comprendre.
Tu essaies de comprendre.
Vous essayez de comprendre.
Il/Elle/On essaie de comprendre.
Ils/Elles essaient de comprendre.

Questions:

  1. Which forms have a spelling change?
  2. What does the spelling change consist of?
  3. Can you formulate a rule for the spelling change?
Show Answers
  1. The spelling change takes place in the singular and in the third-person plural.
  2. The spelling change consists of y changing to i.
  3. In verbs ending in yer, y changes to i before a mute e. The endings of the singular and the third-person plural all have a mute e.

Here are some common verbs ending in yer:

balayer
to sweep
essayer
to try
essayer de faire qqch
to try to do something
payer
to pay
employer
to use
ennuyer
to bore
essuyer
to wipe
nettoyer
to clean
tutoyer
to address someone in the tu form
vouvoyer
to address someone in the vous form

Note that verbs ending in ayer, such as essayer and payer, are sometimes written with the letter y in all forms: je paye, tu payes, etc. However, verbs ending in oyer and uyer always make the change of y to i before a mute e.

  2. What is the stem of the verb sortir?
  3. What happens to the final consonant of the stem in the singular forms of sortir?
  4. What are the endings of the singular form?
Show Answers
  1. The plural forms of sortir do not have the iss of verbs like finir: nous finissons, vous finissez, ils/elles finissent.
  2. The stem of the verb sortir is sort. The stem does not end in s as it does for verbs like finir whose stem is finiss.
  3. The final consonant of the stem (t) drops in the singular forms: je sors, tu sors, il sort.
  4. The endings of the singular form are s, s, and t, as in most verbs whose infinitive does not end in er.

Questions:

  1. How many elements are there in present-tense verb forms?
  2. How many elements are there in past-tense verb forms?
  3. Can you recognize what the first element of the past-tense forms is?
Show Answers
  1. Present-tense verb forms have a single element: travaille, remplissez, perd.
  2. Past-tense verb forms have two elements each: ai travaillé, avez rempli, a perdu.
  3. The first element of the past-tense forms is the present tense of the verb avoir (to have).

The tense most often used to refer to events in the past in French is a compound tense consisting of an auxiliary verb (avoir) and a form of the verb called the “past participle,” or le passé composé. In the next Préparation you will see that some verbs use être rather than avoir to form the passé composé.
